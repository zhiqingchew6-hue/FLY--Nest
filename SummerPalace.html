Summer Palace

5.0 ★★★★★

The Summer Palace, located in the northwest of Beijing, is a vast and well-preserved royal garden. It served as a retreat for emperors of the Qing Dynasty to escape the summer heat and conduct government affairs in a more relaxed environment. Covering over 700 acres, the Summer Palace is renowned for its beautiful natural landscapes, classical Chinese architecture, and harmonious integration of gardens, lakes, and hills. Visitors can enjoy serene scenery, historic buildings, and cultural relics, making it one of the most popular tourist destinations in Beijing.

Top Attraction

The Summer Palace is famous for its combination of natural beauty and exquisite architecture. The Kunming Lake occupies most of the park, providing picturesque reflections of pavilions and bridges. The Longevity Hill offers walking trails with scenic views and many historic structures along the way. Notable attractions within the palace include the Long Corridor, a covered walkway decorated with thousands of paintings; the Marble Boat, a lakeside pavilion made of stone; and the Tower of Buddhist Incense, which stands atop Longevity Hill and offers panoramic views of the surrounding gardens. Visitors can also enjoy boat rides on Kunming Lake or stroll through beautifully landscaped gardens, experiencing the charm of traditional Chinese imperial design.

History

The Summer Palace was originally built in 1750 during the Qing Dynasty and expanded in 1888. It suffered significant damage during wars, particularly the Anglo-French invasion in 1860 and the Boxer Rebellion in 1900, but was later restored to its former glory. As a UNESCO World Heritage site, the Summer Palace reflects the imperial garden design and landscaping techniques of ancient China. It not only served as a summer retreat for emperors but also as a symbol of Chinese garden art, combining natural landscapes with man-made architecture to create a harmonious environment. Today, it attracts millions of visitors each year, offering both historical insight and natural beauty.
