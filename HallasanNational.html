Jeju

Hallasan National Park

3.0 ★★★
Introduction ▶
Introduction Top Attractions History Hotels Traditional Food

Hallasan National Park is located at the center of Jeju Island and is home to Hallasan Mountain, the highest mountain in South Korea. The park is known for its rich biodiversity, volcanic landscapes, and scenic hiking trails. It is a popular destination for nature lovers, hikers, and visitors who want to experience Jeju’s natural beauty.

Top Attraction

Hallasan National Park offers several well-marked hiking trails suitable for different fitness levels. Along the trails, visitors can enjoy volcanic rock formations, forests, and seasonal flowers. At the summit, Baengnokdam Crater Lake provides a stunning natural highlight. Each season brings a different atmosphere to the park, from cherry blossoms in spring to snow-covered landscapes in winter. The park is also home to various plant and animal species unique to Jeju Island.

History

Hallasan Mountain was formed through volcanic activity millions of years ago and has played an important role in shaping Jeju Island’s geography. Due to its ecological value, the area was designated as Hallasan National Park in 1970. Today, it is recognized as part of a UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site, representing Jeju’s volcanic history and natural ecosystem, and remains one of Korea’s most important protected natural areas.
