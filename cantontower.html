Guangzhou

Canton Tower

5.0 ★★★★★

The Canton Tower is Guangzhou's iconic 600-meter tall landmark, a twisted, skeletal structure offering breathtaking city views from its observation decks, restaurants, and thrilling rides like the Sky Drop, symbolizing modern China's blend of history and future ambition. Nicknamed "Xiao Man Yao" (Slim Waist) for its elegant curve, it features an outdoor Skywalk, revolving restaurants, and stunning LED light shows at night, making it a top tourist attraction and engineering marvel built for the 2010 Asian Games. 

Top Attraction

The Canton Tower (Guangzhou TV Astronomical and Sightseeing Tower) was built as a symbol of Guangzhou's modernization for the 2010 Asian Games, designed by IBA (Mark Hemel & Barbara Kuit) to showcase engineering prowess with its unique twisting hyperboloid structure. Construction began in 2005, topped out in 2009, and it opened in September 2010, briefly becoming the world's tallest tower before being surpassed by Tokyo Skytree, serving as a vibrant landmark for tourism, observation, and telecommunications. 

History

The Canton Tower (Guangzhou Tower) is a 600-meter observation and TV tower, a symbol of Guangzhou's modernization, designed by Dutch architects IBA (Information Based Architecture) {Mark Hemel & Barbara Kuit en.wikipedia.org} and Arup, construction started in 2005, topped out in 2009, and opened for the 2010 Asian Games, featuring a unique twisted, waist-like design from rotating ellipses, serving as a major tourist spot with dining, observation decks, and events.
