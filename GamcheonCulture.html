Busan

Gamcheon Culture Village

3.0 ★★★
Introduction ▶
Introduction Top Attractions History Hotels Traditional Food

Gamcheon Culture Village is a colorful and artistic neighborhood located in the western part of Busan. Known for its steep streets, vibrant houses, and creative murals, it has become one of the city’s most popular tourist destinations. The village offers visitors a unique blend of traditional Korean architecture and contemporary street art, making it a perfect spot for photography and cultural exploration.

Top Attraction

Gamcheon Culture Village is famous for its colorful painted houses and narrow winding alleys, where visitors can stroll and discover hidden artworks and sculptures. The village also features art galleries, cafés, souvenir shops, and small museums that showcase local creativity. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Busan from the village’s lookout points and experience interactive art installations throughout the area. Seasonal festivals and cultural events further enhance the lively and artistic atmosphere of the village.

History

Gamcheon Culture Village was originally established in the 1950s as a settlement for Korean War refugees. Over time, the village became densely populated and developed a distinctive terraced structure on the hillside. In the early 2000s, a government-led initiative transformed Gamcheon into a cultural and artistic hub by adding murals, sculptures, and colorful paint. Today, it stands as a symbol of Busan’s creativity and resilience, attracting visitors from around the world.
