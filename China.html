中国美食文化

China

Hot Pot

Hot Pot is a traditional Chinese communal meal where diners cook raw ingredients in a simmering pot of broth placed at the center of the table. The ingredients vary, but often include thinly sliced meats, seafood, tofu, vegetables, and noodles. Broths vary from mild, savory bases to the popular, intensely flavorful, and often fiery, Chongqing-style chili broth. Diners customize their flavor with dipping sauces, often a mix of sesame oil, chili, and garlic chili sauce. Hot Pot emphasizes sharing, customization, and warmth, making it a social and interactive dining experience.

Dan Dan Noodles

Dan Dan Noodles are a classic Sichuan street food known for their spicy, numbing, and savory flavor. The dish is based on a spicy chili oil sauce, often topped with crispy ground pork or beef, preserved vegetables, and crushed peanuts. The combination of the fiery chili, the unique tingling sensation from Sichuan peppercorns, and the savory meat sauce creates a rich and addictive taste. The name originates by street vendors who carried the noodles and sauce on a pole (dan dan) across their shoulders.

Mapo Tofu

Mapo Tofu is a famous Sichuan dish made with soft tofu, ground pork or beef, and a flavorful, spicy sauce made of fermented broad bean paste, chili, garlic, and Sichuan peppercorns. The dish is known for its “mala” flavor, which is a combination of spiciness and the numbing sensation from the peppercorns. Mapo Tofu is usually served hot and pairs perfectly with steamed rice, offering a comforting yet fiery taste that represents authentic Sichuan cuisine.

Beijing Roast Duck

Beijing Roast Duck is internationally renowned, acclaimed for its crisp, golden skin and tender, juicy meat. The duck is seasoned, air-dried, and roasted in a special oven until the skin is perfectly thin and crunchy. Traditionally, the cooked duck is expertly carved table-side by the chef. Diners assemble small pancakes, crisp skin, tender meat and skin in the pancake with the condiments to create flavorful bites. Beijing Roast Duck, with its long culinary history and exquisite taste, and is often considered a culinary symbol of Beijing.
