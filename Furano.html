Hokkaido

Furano & Biei Flower Fields

4.0 ★★★★
Introduction ▶
Introduction Top Attractions History Hotels Traditional Food

Furano and Biei are well-known scenic areas in central Hokkaido, famous for their colorful flower fields and peaceful rural landscapes. These areas are among the most popular natural attractions in Hokkaido, especially during the summer season. With rolling hills, open farmland, and seasonal flowers, Furano and Biei offer visitors a relaxing escape into nature and a chance to enjoy Hokkaido’s countryside beauty.

Top Attraction

The flower fields of Furano and Biei are best known for their vibrant seasonal blooms. In Furano, lavender fields are the main highlight, attracting visitors with their purple colors and pleasant fragrance during summer. Popular spots include Farm Tomita, which features beautifully arranged flower gardens and scenic views. In Biei, the landscape is characterized by gentle hills and patchwork fields. Famous sights such as the Blue Pond (Aoiike) and Patchwork Road offer stunning photo opportunities. Visitors can enjoy cycling, scenic drives, and walking paths while taking in the colorful flower fields and natural scenery.

History

Furano and Biei were originally agricultural areas, developed for farming and crop cultivation. Over time, farmers began planting flowers to improve the landscape and attract visitors, especially during the summer months. As tourism grew, the flower fields became a symbol of Hokkaido’s natural beauty. Today, Furano and Biei are internationally recognized for their seasonal scenery, representing the harmony between agriculture, nature, and tourism in the region.
