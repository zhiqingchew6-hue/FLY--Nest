Yu Garden

5.0 ★★★★★
Introduction ▶
Introduction Top Attractions History Hotels Traditional Food

Yu Garden is a famous classical Chinese garden located in the Old City of Shanghai. It is one of the best-preserved traditional gardens in the city and offers visitors a peaceful escape from the surrounding urban environment. The garden is known for its elegant layout, traditional pavilions, rockeries, ponds, and bridges. Yu Garden reflects the beauty and philosophy of classical Chinese garden design, making it an important cultural and historical attraction in Shanghai.

Top Attraction

Yu Garden features many beautifully designed areas that highlight traditional Chinese architecture and landscaping. One of the most iconic sights is the Exquisite Jade Rock, a unique limestone rock with many holes and a long history. The Mid-Lake Pavilion and the Nine-Turn Bridge are also major attractions, offering picturesque views and excellent photo opportunities. Visitors can explore traditional halls, courtyards, and scenic pathways throughout the garden. Outside the garden, the surrounding Yuyuan Bazaar is filled with shops and food stalls selling local snacks, souvenirs, and traditional handicrafts, allowing visitors to experience Shanghai’s local culture and cuisine.

History

Yu Garden was originally built in 1559 during the Ming Dynasty by a government official named Pan Yunduan as a private garden for his parents. The garden took nearly 20 years to complete and was designed to reflect harmony between nature and architecture. Over the centuries, Yu Garden suffered damage during wars and periods of social unrest but was restored several times. Today, it is a protected cultural site and remains a symbol of traditional Chinese garden art, preserving the historical charm of old Shanghai while welcoming visitors from around the world.
