Korea

Korean BBQ

Korean BBQ is a popular style of dining where diners grill their own meat, typically beef, pork, or chicken, at the table. The meat is often marinated in a variety of sauces, such as soy-based or spicy gochujang marinades, giving it rich and savory flavors. Korean BBQ is commonly served with side dishes called banchan, including kimchi, pickled vegetables, and dipping sauces. Lettuce or perilla leaves are used to wrap the grilled meat along with rice and condiments, creating a flavorful bite. The interactive grilling experience, variety of meats, and bold flavors make Korean BBQ a social and festive meal.

Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is a classic Korean street food made from soft, chewy rice cakes cooked in a spicy and slightly sweet red chili paste sauce called gochujang. Often combined with fish cakes, boiled eggs, vegetables, and scallions, it delivers a comforting, bold, and satisfying flavor. Tteokbokki is known for its chewy texture and its rich, savory, and sweet-spicy sauce. It is commonly enjoyed as a snack, casual meal, or festival food, and has become one of Korea’s most iconic street dishes.

Budae Jjigae

Budae Jjigae, or “Army Stew,” is a hearty Korean hot pot dish that originated during the post-Korean War period, when food shortages led people to creatively use surplus ingredients from U.S. military bases. It combines a mix of processed meats such as spam, hot dogs, and sausages with kimchi, tofu, ramen noodles, vegetables, and gochujang-based broth. The dish is spicy, savory, and deeply comforting, often cooked at the table in a communal pot, making it perfect for sharing. Today, Budae Jjigae is a beloved Korean comfort food enjoyed by families and friends alike.
